Cats: Why you should get one

ASPCA Statistics on Animal shelters:

Adopting a cat from a shelter is very important; approximately "6.5 million companion animals" enter animal shelters in the U.S. annually, and approximately 3.2 million were cats. Adopting from a shelter will help these animals find a safe, loving home. If you adopt a cat from a shelter, then you will help break the pattern of pet overpopulation. There are insufficient homes for all these animals. Adopting from shelters will help break this overpopulation cycle.

Source: ASPCA

Great Reasons to get a cat:

  • They are actually known for reducing anxiety and depression
  • Owning a cat is actually better for the environment. Studies show that because cats eat less in general and are more likely to eat fish which have a low carbon footprint.
  • Research shows that cat owners are more intelligent
  • Cats are actually good for your heart because research shows they reduce stress.
  • Cats actually bathe themselves, so they are very low-maintenance in consideration of how independant, yet loving they are.
  • Cats are very quiet overall, which is good for everyone.

    Checklist for materials you should buy if you get a cat:

  • Cats, like all living things, require a source of energy. As a pet owner, it is your responsibility to take care of your cat.

    • For kittens, purchase foods with "Complete and Balanced Nutrition" and AAFCO animal feeding tested statement

    For adult cats, canned foods are the best option

  • A litterbox: more specifically, plant-based litter.
  • You will need food and water bowls: the most recommendable types of bowls to purchase would be ceramic or stainless steel bowls
  • A cat carrier: needed whenever you take your cat anywhere
